I’m a professor of informatics in the Faculty of Science and the Faculty of Medicine and Health Sciences at the University of Sherbrooke since 2022, I develop interdisciplinary approaches at the intersection of computer science and digital health. I am a co-holder of the Chair in Digital Health supported by Quebec’s MEIE and the recipient of NSERC Discovery grant. 
My research focuses on several aspects of computer science, including the modeling of temporal databases, database definition and query languages using ontologies, the generation of knowledge graphs, and the integration of heterogeneous data. Since 2023, I have organized the Interdisciplinary Summer School in Digital Health (EINS), which introduces participants to the challenges of digitizing health data. Additionally, I have served as a mentor in the UdeS Mentoring Program for Women in Science and Engineering (since 2024) and the Loran Foundation (since 2025).
