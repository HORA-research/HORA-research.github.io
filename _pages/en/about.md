Department of Medicine, Faculty of Medicine and Health Sciences <br>
Department of Computer science, Faculty of Sciences <br>
<a href='https://www.usherbrooke.ca'>Université de Sherbrooke</a>. Sherbrooke (Québec) CANADA.

A wide variety of data is generated in different places at different times. In addition, independent systems use various data analysis methods for everyday activities to improve their decision-making, leading to “application silos”. However, to exchange data or results to make informed decisions at the right time, these systems need to talk to and understand each other. 
To help analysts and managers make the right decisions at the right time, it is essential to implement an integrated solution that allows different sources to be documented, mapped and queried. The integrated solution approach is innovative in that the resolution of semantic and temporal heterogeneity problems will be based on a single model and algorithms that handle the generation of appropriate codes. In this way, the approach will enable interoperable and higher-quality projects to be carried out at lower cost, contributing both to the advancement of science and to the implementation of decision support systems. 

The research group focuses on developing methods and tools for modeling and historicizing databases in order to enable : 
- (O1) linking of data from multiple sources to a knowledge model guaranteeing uniform interpretation; 
- (O2) temporal tracking of data evolution offering full traceability of changes;
- (O3) formulation of queries on the linked model with an intuitive language making the data easily accessible.
